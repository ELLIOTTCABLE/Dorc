## Findings
- Clang has THREE distinct suppression layers, not one — and they map cleanly onto Dorc's needs:
  1. **User-directed suppression** (`BugSuppression.cpp`, [B-llvm-bugsuppression-2025]): a `[[clang::suppress]]` attribute on a decl/stmt creates a `SourceRange`; a bug is suppressed iff `fullyContains(suppressionRange, bugRange)`. Range-containment, lexical-parent-walk so an attribute on a class covers inline methods. This is the ADMIN-knob analog (a human says "shut up about this region"), NOT cause-dedup.
  2. **Whole-report heuristic suppression** (`LikelyFalsePositiveSuppressionBRVisitor::finalizeVisitor`, [A-llvm-bugreportervisitors-2025]): a hardcoded denylist of known-imprecision sites. If the bug's stack-frame decl is in `std::`, or in classes `list`/`basic_string`/`shared_ptr`/`__independent_bits_engine`, or in `sys/queue.h` macros — `BR.markInvalid(getTag(), nullptr)` kills the ENTIRE report. This is the "minimum shippable suppression set" pattern: don't model the imprecision, just denylist the few constructs the analyzer is known to be wrong about. Tiny, ~100 lines, hand-curated from bug reports.
  3. **Path-piece pruning / dedup** (`removeRedundantMsgs`, `removeUnneededCalls`, `eventsDescribeSameCondition` in `BugReporter.cpp`, [A-llvm-bugreporter-2025]): post-analysis re-walk of the diagnostic path that (a) DEDUPS two pieces at the SAME location emitted by two different visitors, keeping the more-specific one via a fixed preference order; (b) PRUNES call/macro subpaths that contain "nothing interesting".
- The dedup-to-root mechanism IS interestingness-tracking: `removeUnneededCalls` keeps a path piece only if `containsSomethingInteresting` (recursively), and `event.isPrunable()` defaults TRUE — events are thrown away wholesale unless a visitor marked them non-prunable (`setPrunable(false)`) or they touch an `isInteresting()` symbol/region. This is the closest Clang analog to Dorc's "⊤ carries a cause-pointer; only the cause-site note is interesting": Clang's reports survive only along the chain marked interesting from the bug site backward.
- `eventsDescribeSameCondition`: when ConditionBRVisitor and TrackConstraintBRVisitor BOTH emit a note at the identical `getLocation()`, keep ConditionBRVisitor's (unless it's the generic fallback msg) and drop the other. Concrete "two-engines-one-fact → one note" precedent — exactly Dorc's per-⊤-operand multiplication shape, where N consumers describe the same ⊤.
