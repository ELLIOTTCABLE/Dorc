## The landscape (shape of the answer)
The sweep converges on a coherent practice-set; the variance lives in *how far Dorc commits*, not in *what the practices are*. Two human-sharpened crux points reshape it: (1) the tier boundary that matters is a **2×2 quadrant**, not a line (`axis-quadrant`); (2) "best-effort" must mean *maximal* rigor, not relaxed rigor (`concl-rigor`).

## Axes the decision turns on (priority order: maintainability > simplicity > validation > perf)
- **`axis-dst-cost` (validation ⟂ maintainability/simplicity):** full DST maximizes validation but its single-thread / async-IO / everything-through-the-seam constraints tax #1/#2 hard. The *seam alone* is cheap; the *simulator* is expensive. (deferred decision; Front A informs it.)
- **`axis-quadrant` (THE CONTAINERIZABILITY QUADRANT — human-sharpened, the crux of Front B):** not a 1-D fast/slow line but {fast,slow} × {containerizable, non-containerizable}. The hard axis is *containerizability* — can the behaviour be fully exercised inside a localhost network-of-containers, or is it too "real"? *Containerizable:* ssh to a box, ship a compiled probe, integrate results into the plan. *Non-containerizable:* reconfigure sshd and check the tunnel renegotiates/restores state. This seam matches **none** of Dorc's known seams — not invoked-tool, not POSIX-construct, not domain-of-control (books *and* oracles hit it), not execution-time (a containerizable test can be slow-but-deterministic; a non-containerizable one can be instant-by-network-standards). Unintuitive; may need constant tracking. **Driving question for Front B: do other shops *infer* this boundary, or *create + enforce* it artificially (tags / DSL / metadata)?** [A-puppet-litmus-concepts-2024] (`notes/076` perf; `notes/077` seam.)
- **`axis-infer` (enforce ⟂ annotate):** can Dorc *infer* a tier boundary (network-reach; or harder, containerizability) from AST/taint and mechanically gate it — the way Google's sandbox polices test *size* [A-google-test-sizes-2010] — or must it be hand-declared like everyone else? This is where the static-analyzer meets the test-tiering; `axis-quadrant` is the hard instance of it.
- **`axis-platform` (cross-platform — human constraint, see AGENTS):** dev is macOS/Windows/Linux. Linux-kernel-specific mechanisms (tc/netem, iptables, netns) **cannot be load-bearing in the hot-development TDD loop**; at most they belong in CI/end-of-round tests. Anything depending on Linux-only tooling is effectively a "slow test" *regardless of wall-clock*. The fast inner loop must use OS-portable mechanisms (seam-level mock, in-process sim, userspace proxy).

## Conclusion call-outs (human-flagged — must surface in the eventual conclusion)
- **`concl-quadrant` (from f9/`axis-quadrant`; Front B confirmed — FRONTLOAD):** the containerizability boundary is **hand-annotated by everyone** (Bazel tags, K8s `[Serial]`/`[Disruptive]` labels, kselftest self-skip); **nobody statically infers it**, and the academic frontier (`saflate`) infers only the *network* sub-axis, *dynamically*, because hand-annotation of network-deps is unreliable. Do not conflate two objects: **(A)** how Dorc tiers its **own (Rust) tests** — Rust-annotated, infer-answer mostly-no-like-everyone, with a Linux-only seccomp/sandbox *escape-detector* in CI and the **portable DST seam** as the low-tier enforcer; **(B)** inferring a **user sh leaf's** containerizability — the product-feature where effect-analysis + oracle-knowledge + "spelled in sh"/`kOOB` live, **oracle-dependent and parked**. (B) is the genuine-novelty bet; (A) is conventional. `plans/077`'s seccomp is a *product* tool (detects network in user leaves), scoped by the human as potentially-platform-limited DX for oracle authors — *not* an already-banked self-test primitive (`axis-platform`: Linux-only). Nothing here decided.
- **`concl-rigor` (from f13):** "best-effort" is NOT an escape-hatch. For Dorc it means provable-algorithm-level rigor ("best-in-the-universe effort") so the *user* needn't be correct; the soundness ceiling exists only to bound dangerous algorithmic assumptions and to honestly acknowledge user-facing imperfection arising from the day-1 oracle contract. Front-load this; testing rigor must be maximal, never relaxed by appeal to "we're best-effort."
- **`concl-tdd` (from f6):** target TDD posture = static-typing-discipline + contracts/boundary-testing + fuzzing + tests-first (spanning unit/integration/types conceptually). 100%-unit-coverage is an antipattern. Fuzzing ↔ property-based testing (f12) is the natural bridge.
- **`concl-jepsen` (from f4):** decide and state whether Jepsen/Maelstrom are prior-art-to-mine or tools-to-adopt.
