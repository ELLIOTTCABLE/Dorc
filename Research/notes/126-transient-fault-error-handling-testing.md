## Findings

- f39. **Jepsen's core op model IS the three-valued verdict — independent convergence with `notes/112` f50.** Every completion is `:ok` (succeeded), `:fail` (definitely did not happen), or `:info` (we are not sure — a thrown exception/timeout auto-converts to `:info`, "allow that it might or might not have taken place") [A-jepsen-client-op-model-2026]. That is True/False/**Unknown** as the *native unit* of a distributed-correctness harness. **How Dorc tests its verdict:** inject a timeout / drop-after-mutate at the `dorc_exec` seam, assert the orchestrator records **Unknown**, not a false ok/fail. +SURE.
- f40. **`concl-jepsen`: prior-art-to-mine primarily; optionally a real-cluster CI tier; NOT the hot loop, and its checkers don't fit Dorc.** Jepsen's *shape* maps onto Dorc remarkably — a control node SSHes into target nodes (any machine with TCP+SSH+sudo), runs os/db/client/generator/nemesis/checker, and injects partitions via `iptables`/`tc` [A-jepsen-readme-architecture-2026][A-jepsen-net-clj-source-2026]. But three blockers: (a) its built-in **checkers are consistency models** (linearizability/serializability/causal, via Knossos/Elle) — Dorc's correctness is "did the host reach desired state," a *different* oracle, so you would write a **custom checker/model**; (b) **Clojure/JVM adoption cost** (WarpStream: "none of our engineers had to learn Clojure"); (c) it is the **slow real-cluster tier**, while the DST seam gives deterministic/portable/fast fault-injection Jepsen cannot. Most reusable: the `:ok/:fail/:info` model, the generator→nemesis→checker architecture, the fault catalogue, "prove presence not absence." Maelstrom fits *less* (Dorc is a controller, not a peer-node speaking a protocol over stdin/stdout). (Note the `jepsen.antithesis` bridge — the two lineages are converging.) +SURE→~SUSPECT.
- f41. **Sometimes-assertions are the reachability-half answer to "is my fault-injection actually working?"** Antithesis's model (now mirrored cross-language in moonpool / turso / ordeal): a *sometimes-assertion* asserts a state IS reachable, so a sim that never exercises the error path **fails loudly** [B-antithesis-sometimes-assertions-2026]. Reserve `assert_always!` for paths you must hold; use `assert_sometimes!`/`reachable` to prove the unhappy path was hit. Dorc should assert-sometimes the host-dropped-after-mutate / Unknown path is reached — the partial answer to the Eaton/Will-Wilson "terrifyingly easy to build a DST that explores nothing" worry (notes/122). +SURE.
- f42. **(counter-thesis resolved) The DST seam subsumes *most* of Dorc's fault-injection; real netem/iptables/Jepsen tests the transport + real tool, which for Dorc is mostly the *mocked edge* (not Dorc's code).** Synthetic adverse-outcome injection at `dorc_exec` (timeout / drop-after-mutate / partition / reorder) is deterministic, portable, hot-loop, and tests the orchestrator's *logic*. Real-fault-injection only adds value for transport-integration (does Dorc's ssh-wrapping survive a real mid-stream drop?) and end-to-end assurance — a slow high-tier CI complement, not the primary. **So Front C's apparatus ≈ Front A's seam + adverse outcomes + Sometimes-assertions + a custom (Jepsen-style) checker.** Front A and Front C unify at the seam. ~SUSPECT→+SURE.
- f43. **Testing error-PROVENANCE under faults (bridge to round-11).** Jepsen's model-based checker (compare observed *history* against a *model* of legal behaviour) = Dorc's checker compares observed (host-states + reported verdicts + provenance) against a model of what the book *should* have produced [A-jepsen-etcd-3-4-3-2020]. So: inject a fault, then assert the resulting error carries correct provenance — `loc-host`, the leaf-ID, the **Unknown** verdict, the depends-on edge (`notes/110`–`113`). And the **retry-safety hazard**: under Unknown a retry must not double-apply — test by injecting drop-after-mutate, retrying, and asserting no double-apply (idempotency, `notes/120` f3, as a *test oracle* here even though idempotency-as-product is parked). +SURE.
- f44. **Portable fault-injection ladder (`axis-platform`).** seam-synthetic (hot loop, all-OS, deterministic) → Toxiproxy userspace TCP proxy (real sockets, cross-platform, CI-ish — cut-then-restore between containers [B-testcontainers-toxiproxy-2024]) → `tc`/netem + `iptables` (real kernel faults, Linux-only, slow CI [A-tc-netem-network-emulator-2026]) → full Jepsen (real cluster, Linux, Clojure). The hot loop must live at the portable top; kernel-altitude is CI-only (notes/120 f10, f18). +SURE.
